Arjun's Story

Month 1 is a blur. You're adjusting to everything — new city, new campus, new routine. The last thing on your mind is gear.

Arjun figured he'd sort it out later. He had his laptop, his phone, and a charger. That was enough, right?

Week 1. His phone dies on the way to class. He misses his bus stop because Google Maps cuts out. He arrives late, flustered, and sits in the back.

Week 2. He buys cheap earbuds from a corner store. One side stops working in three days.

Week 3. His neck starts aching. He's been hunching over his laptop on a flat desk for six hours a day. His posture is a disaster.

Month 2. His backpack strap snaps. He's been carrying a 6kg load in a $20 bag he brought from home.

Month 3. He's studying late but can't turn on the overhead light — his roommate is asleep. He uses his phone flashlight. His eyes hurt.

By Month 3, Arjun had spent $180 replacing broken gear, fixing problems that never needed to happen.

💡
Tip: Your phone is your lifeline here — bus routes, maps, family messages, everything runs through it. Never leave without a charged power bank. It's not optional; it's infrastructure.

Priya's Story

Priya made a list before she landed. Not a wishlist — a priority list. She knew she couldn't buy everything at once, so she mapped it by week.

Week 1. Power bank and earbuds. $60 total. Her phone never dies in transit. She listens to lectures on the bus. She arrives to every class on time.

💡
Tip: If you're in a shared room or studying in a loud library, ANC earbuds are not a luxury — they're focus protection. One pair, bought right, lasts all four years. Your roommate's 1am YouTube habit becomes your problem without them.

Week 2. Laptop stand and backpack. $85 total. Her screen is at eye level. Her back is straight. Her laptop is protected.

💡
Tip: Your body keeps score. Hunching over a laptop for 3-hour study sessions adds up fast. Keep your screen at eye level, sit with your back straight, and if you're studying from your mattress in the first few weeks, a small laptop lap desk makes a real difference until you're properly set up.

Month 2. A windproof compact umbrella. $22. She has never arrived to class wet.

💡
Tip: Invest in at least one good backpack with thick padded straps and a dedicated laptop compartment. You'll be throwing it down, picking it up, and wearing it for 8-hour days. The last thing you want is a scratched laptop because your bag had no padding.
💡
Tip: Canadian weather doesn't warn you. It can go from sunny to sideways rain in 20 minutes. A compact windproof umbrella that lives in your bag pocket means you always arrive dry — without ever thinking about it.

Month 3. A surge-protected power bar and a small desk lamp. $45 total.

💡
Tip: One surge-protected power bar fixes the outlet problem permanently — six plugs, one cable, clean desk. And a small desk lamp changes late-night studying completely. Your roommate sleeps, you keep grinding. Different goals, same room — the lamp makes it work.

By Month 3, Priya had spent $212 total — on gear that still works in Year 2.

Essential Gear — What to Buy & When

Seven items. Buy in order of daily impact — not all at once.

🔋 Power Bank

Why: Your phone is your transit pass, your map, your family line. Dead phone = missed bus, missed class, missed messages.
What to look for: 10,000–20,000mAh · USB-C · fast charge · fits in bag pocket
What to avoid: Anything under 10,000mAh · no-brand options with no reviews
Arjun's mistake: Borrowed his roommate's every other day for 3 weeks
Priya's pick: Anker 10,000mAh slim — charged her phone twice on one charge
Budget: $35–$55

Anker Power Bank →

🎧 Earbuds / Headphones

Why: Study sessions, commutes, lectures on the go — you need reliable audio with decent noise isolation.
What to look for: ANC (Active Noise Cancellation) · 20+ hour battery · comfortable for long sessions
What to avoid: Cheap store-brand earbuds · wired-only options
Arjun's mistake: $12 corner store earbuds. One side died in 3 days.
Priya's pick: Mid-range ANC earbuds — still working 2 years later
Budget: $40–$150

Budget pick →Premium pick →

💻 Laptop Stand

Why: Laptop flat on desk = screen at shoulder height = you hunched forward = neck and back pain by Month 2. A foldable stand fixes it permanently — and it fits in your bag.
What to look for: Adjustable height · foldable for bag · lightweight
What to avoid: Fixed-height stands · bulky non-portable stands
Arjun's mistake: Flat laptop on desk for 3 months. Physiotherapy by Month 4.
Priya's pick: $35 foldable laptop stand. Laptop at eye level from Day 1.
Budget: $25–$45

Laptop Stand →Bed Desk →

🎒 Backpack

Why: You'll carry this 5 days a week for 4 years. Laptop, chargers, books, water bottle — it adds up to 5–8kg daily.
What to look for: Padded laptop compartment · thick shoulder straps · water resistant · 20–30L
What to avoid: Fashion backpacks with no structure · bags without laptop sleeves
Arjun's mistake: $20 bag from home. Strap snapped Month 2. Laptop scratched.
Priya's pick: Mid-range backpack with laptop sleeve — still going strong
Budget: $50–$90

Backpack (Option 1) →

☂️ Compact Umbrella

Why: Canadian weather is unpredictable. Rain, snow, sleet — sometimes all in one day. You will get caught without one.
What to look for: Windproof · compact enough for bag side pocket · auto open/close
What to avoid: Large golf umbrellas · cheap ones that flip inside out in wind
Arjun's mistake: Got soaked 4 times before buying one in Month 3.
Priya's pick: Repel windproof travel umbrella — fits in her bag, survived a storm
Budget: $20–$30

Repel Windproof Umbrella →

🔌 Surge-Protected Power Bar

Why: Dorm rooms have 1–2 outlets for 6+ devices. A good power bar with surge protection means your laptop, phone, earbuds, and lamp are all covered — and protected.
What to look for: Surge protection · 6+ outlets · USB-A and USB-C ports · flat plug
What to avoid: Cheap bars with no surge protection · round plugs (hard to use against walls)
Arjun's mistake: Used a single outlet with a flimsy splitter. It sparked once.
Priya's pick: 6-outlet surge bar with 2 USB ports — one cable, whole desk powered
Budget: $25–$40

Surge Power Bar →

💡 Desk Lamp

Why: Overhead dorm lights are harsh or unusable when your roommate sleeps. A warm LED desk lamp means you can study at midnight without waking anyone.
What to look for: LED · adjustable brightness · USB charging port on base · compact
What to avoid: Bulky floor lamps · halogen lights · anything without brightness control
Arjun's mistake: Used phone flashlight for 3 weeks. Eyes hurt constantly.
Priya's pick: Small LED lamp with USB port — charged her phone while she studied
Budget: $20–$35
