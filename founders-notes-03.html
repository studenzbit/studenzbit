The Founder's Notes · May 2026
The Thought

A friend got a job offer in a WhatsApp group. He sent his SIN, study permit, and passport photos before he ever stepped foot in the store. By day two he was gone — unpaid, documents compromised, and angrier than he had ever been in Canada.

The Story

The job came through a WhatsApp group. It happens all the time here.

He was told he was selected. Just send your SIN, your study permit, and a photo of your passport to move forward. He was eager. Four months of job hunting does that to you. He sent everything.

The first day he showed up, he was told training is two weeks and it is unpaid. No company uniform during training either, so go buy a shirt that fits the dress code. He bought the shirt. He worked the first day. He never once spoke to the owner. Just the manager, who kept things moving and kept him away from asking real questions.

On day two he started pushing. He wanted to talk to the owner directly. After an argument the manager finally connected them. The owner said it plainly: training is unpaid. If you do not like it, leave the store right now.

He left.

But it was not just the money. The work environment was designed to break him. He was spoken to rudely from day one, belittled in front of others, made to feel like he should be grateful just to be there. That is not accidental. That is the strategy. Make the person feel so small and so unwelcome that they walk out on their own before the two weeks are up. That way the owner never has to fire anyone. The student just disappears, unpaid, and a new one comes in next week.

What he did not know yet, and what I want you to know right now, is that this is not rare. There are shop owners in this country, many of them immigrants themselves, who have turned this into a system. They know a new international student is desperate, does not know their rights, and will work two weeks for free hoping to get the job. After two weeks they tell you that you are not the right fit. Then they bring in the next person.

The rules at this store were also written to steal from you quietly. Work till 9:45pm, get paid till 9:00. Start at 10:00am, get paid from 11:00. Fifteen minutes here, an hour there. It adds up and they are counting on you not knowing it is illegal.

And your documents. Your SIN, your study permit, your passport photos are now with someone you met through a WhatsApp group. They cannot touch your employment record. But with your SIN and ID they can apply for credit cards in your name. Finance a phone or a laptop on monthly installments in your name. This is not an accusation of what this specific person did. This is what becomes possible when someone has your documents. Know the risk.

This happened at a place most people in this city visit at least once a week. You would recognize the name.

There are shop owners in this country who have turned unpaid training into a system. Two weeks free, then you are not the right fit, then the next person comes in. They are counting on your desperation and your silence.

The Lesson

Never share your SIN, study permit, or passport until you have a written offer letter from an official company email address. A WhatsApp message is not an offer. A phone call is not an offer. A letter from a real email with the company name is an offer. If you worked somewhere and were not paid correctly, keep every text message, every job posting screenshot, your Google Timeline location records showing you were there, and every communication. Then file a claim with your province's employment standards office. For Ontario, file at Ontario Employment Standards — Filing a Claim. Search your province's official government website if you are elsewhere. You are protected by law in this country even as an international student. Most people who take advantage of you are counting on you not knowing that.
